1. Use resistant varieties. Contact your local agriculture office for up-to-date lists of varieties available.
2. Keep fields clean.
3. Remove weeds and weedy rice in the field and nearby areas to remove alternate hosts that allow the fungus to survive and infect new rice crops.
4. Use balanced nutrients; make sure that adequate potassium is used.
5. If narrow brown spot poses a risk to the field, spray propiconazole at booting to heading stages.
